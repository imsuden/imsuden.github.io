Creative commons

Adrian is right: a license is a license. It lays down what you can and can't do with something, while asserting ownership and authorship. I guess you could have a license that says a piece of work is usable as long as you're not [insert list of the man's companies].

Yes, it is interesting how some interpret a license from an anti–big–business rather free culture perspective. Or rather, that is the entirely right interpretation of a license, but it is more important to fight big business than make your work freely usable by anyone.
